Homemade Blueberry Wine

Enjoy a rich, flavorful homemade blueberry wine with this easy-to-follow recipe, perfect for any wine enthusiast.

Tip: Blending the blueberries beforehand enhances the flavor and speeds up the fermentation process.

Ingredients

  • 4-5 pounds of frozen blueberries (thawed)
  • 2 pounds of sugar (or adjust to taste)
  • 1 gallon of water
  • 1/4 tsp wine yeast (like Lalvin EC-1118)
  • 1 Campden tablet (optional)
  • 1 tsp yeast nutrient
  • 1 tsp pectic enzyme (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp acid blend (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp potassium sorbate (for stabilization)

Equipment

  • Primary fermentation bucket
  • Sanitized funnel
  • Strainer or cheesecloth
  • Hydrometer (optional)
  • Siphon
  • Glass carboy (for secondary fermentation)

Instructions

1. Sanitize Equipment: Ensure all equipment is sanitized properly to avoid contamination.
2. Prepare Blueberries: Thaw and blend the blueberries, then pour them into the fermentation bucket.
3. Add Sugar and Water: Dissolve sugar in warm water and add to blueberries, then add remaining water to make 1 gallon. Mix well.
4. Add Nutrients: Stir in yeast nutrient, pectic enzyme, and acid blend (if using).
5. Optional - Sterilize: Add a crushed Campden tablet and let sit for 24 hours.
6. Add Yeast: Sprinkle yeast on top, then cover with plastic wrap or lid fitted with an airlock.
7. Primary Fermentation: Let the mixture ferment for 5-7 days, stirring daily.
8. Strain and Rack: After a week, strain out the solids and siphon the liquid into a carboy.
9. Secondary Fermentation: Fit carboy with airlock, let ferment for 4-6 weeks in a cool, dark place.
10. Rack and Stabilize: After fermentation, siphon into a clean carboy and add potassium sorbate if needed.
11. Bottle and Age: Siphon into bottles, cork, and age for 6-12 months for best flavor.
Enjoy! After aging, your blueberry wine is ready to enjoy. The flavor will improve with time.
