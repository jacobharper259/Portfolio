Apple Cinnamon Wine Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 gallon of 100% apple juice (no preservatives)
  • 1 cup sugar (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon wine yeast (Lalvin EC-1118 or Red Star Champagne)
  • 1/2 teaspoon yeast nutrient (optional)
  • Sanitized 1-gallon carboy or fermenter with an airlock

Instructions

  1. Sanitize all equipment to avoid unwanted bacteria or wild yeast.
  2. Pour the apple juice into the carboy or fermenter, leaving 3-4 inches of space at the top.
  3. Dissolve the sugar in a small amount of warm water (if using) and add to the apple juice with cinnamon powder.
  4. Add yeast nutrient (if using) and sprinkle the wine yeast on top of the juice mixture without stirring.
  5. Attach the airlock to the fermenter.
  6. Primary Fermentation (1-2 Weeks): Place in a cool, dark area (65–75°F). Fermentation should start in 24-48 hours, and bubbling should be visible. Let ferment until bubbling slows.
  7. Secondary Fermentation (2-4 Weeks): Siphon (rack) the wine into a clean carboy, leaving sediment behind. Seal with airlock and let sit for 2-4 weeks to clarify.
  8. Taste and adjust cinnamon flavor if needed. You can add a cinnamon stick for a stronger flavor.
  9. Bottling: Once fermentation has stopped, siphon into sanitized bottles, leaving sediment behind.
  10. Aging (Optional): Age for a few months for a richer flavor, though it can be enjoyed immediately.

Enjoy your homemade apple cinnamon wine!
